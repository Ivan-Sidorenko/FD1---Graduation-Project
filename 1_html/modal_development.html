  • What are they, who are suitable for
    and how do bionic prostheses work?

    The bionic prosthesis, which is also bioelectric and myoelectric, works by reading the special potential sensors that produce during the tension of the preserved muscle tissue of the arm with special myo-sensors. Myo sensors, which ensure the correct reading of this electrical potential, consist of sensitive electrodes. They transmit the read signal to the microprocessor, the brain of the entire prosthesis, which performs the processing of the received information using computer algorithms. As a result, the microprocessor, relying on the received signal, forms commands in a split second and sends them to the engines (motors), which set in motion the active parts of the prosthesis.

    At the moment, bionic prostheses are divided into two types:

    Single grip

    Multi-grip

    Bionic prostheses are suitable for adult users with injuries
    at the level of the forearm and are indicated in the following cases:


    Congenital malformations by type of stump at all levels;
    Amputation stumps at all levels;
    On a short and long stump and after isolation in the wrist joint.

Modern bionic prostheses

Let's look first at what “bionic prostheses” are. Bionics is an applied science that combines biology and technology. Wildlife helps scientists find solutions for technical devices. There are biological bionics, which studies processes in biological systems, theoretical, which builds mathematical models of these processes and technical. We now need technical bionics: it applies theoretical bionics models to solve engineering problems, as is the case with prostheses.

  • Previously, specialists implied by “bionic” prostheses such devices that are similar to the part of the body that they replace. From the point of view of modern concepts, these prostheses are those that are controlled by electronics and biocurrents, that is, they use myography or an encephalogram.

    The British company RSLSteeper, which currently has about 90 years of experience in prosthetics, introduced the BeBionic bionic prosthesis to the international market in 2010. At that time, an artificial arm for an adult had only four functional grips, but it already allowed you to eat, drink, print, turn the key in the lock, use an ATM and hold small objects. Using the device, the user can break eggs and hold a disposable cup in his hand - because even the force of pressing is regulated by commands removed by sensors from the muscles.

    One of the most serious hand prosthetics players in the world is Bebionic. Often in the media they say that this company made the first bionic prosthesis.
