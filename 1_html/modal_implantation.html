The main reason for this is the imperfection of the mechanism

Four main types of human interaction with the prosthesis can be distinguished:

The first, most radical - various kinds of implants in the motor and sensory zones of the cerebral cortex. Such an interface has the same disadvantages as a transplanted arm. Implants in the brain are especially appropriate when, for whatever reason, the connection between the brain and the arm is broken. In other cases, it is worthwhile to additionally assess the benefit / risk of using such an interface.

The second control method is the use of electroencephalography (EEG). The EEG method is based on recording the bioelectrical activity of the brain that occurs as a result of the spread of the action potential through neurons. The method is considered promising, but has a number of technical difficulties that prevent the appearance of an interface based on it. Firstly, because of the peculiarities of registering a brain activity map, the system needs to be “trained” again when moving the electrodes. And secondly, the signal itself is very unstable to various kinds of electrical interference and interference.

Third: implantation of electrodes to peripheral neurons in the remaining part of the arm. This method has all the same problems as transplantation and brain implants, and it also requires long-term and individual work of doctors.

And the last type of interface is electromyography (EMG). Its simplest implementation - trigger - is used in mechanical prostheses, controlling the compression or expansion of the hand. In bionic prostheses, the exact same control system is introduced. But, as already mentioned, EMG in them is used only for two degrees of freedom - flexion and extension of the fingers. Also, a third degree of freedom can be added to them - the simultaneous tension of both muscles, on which EMG activity is measured.
